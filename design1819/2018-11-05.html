Beams, undetermined systems

Beams are structural elements characterized by a dimension much larger than the others and that are mostly interested by bending moments and shear forces.
Today we studied how the elastic line method can be used to study statically overdetermined systems (where more than a unique statical solution is possible) using the equations of the elastic line, deducing the method of forces as applied to continuous beams.

Today we also briefly touched the subject of stability of equilibrium.
